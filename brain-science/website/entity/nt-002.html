Serotonin modulates virtually every brain function. 95% of the body's serotonin is in the gut. SSRIs are the most prescribed psychiatric drugs. Recent research shows psilocybin (5-HT2A agonist) produces rapid antidepressant effects. 14 serotonin receptor subtypes mediate diverse effects from mood to gut motility.
Type
Indolamine
Function
Mood regulation, sleep, appetite, pain, social behavior, impulsivity
Associated Regions
Raphe nuclei (brainstem), widespread projections to cortex, limbic system, spinal cord
Disorders
Depression, anxiety, OCD, PTSD, migraine, irritable bowel syndrome
Drugs Targeting
SSRIs (fluoxetine, sertraline), SNRIs, triptans, LSD/psilocybin, ondansetron
